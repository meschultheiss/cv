09/16		 Accounting for Dynamics in Pedestrian multi-class speed-density relationship, TRANSP-OR
Read more...

Accounting for heterogeneity and dynamics in fundamental speed-density relationship. Development of a mixture model through a data-driven approach based on the case study of Lausanne Train Station. A latent-class mixture model was developed, structured with a Class Membership Model and a Class Specific Model.

Three latent-classes yield very distinct clusters of pedestrians over the time. Pedestrian can be sorted in fast, slow, and average-speed class.
For partial fulfillment of the requirements of the Civil Engineer at EPFL.

 EPFL, Lausanne, CH modeling (mixture) data analysis
12/16
09/16		 Introducing Priority rules in Passenger Assignment Models, TRANSP-OR
Read more...

Implementation of priority rules in a static passenger-centric transit assignment model. Measurement and qualitative understanding of passenger inconvenience in the multi-objective function.

Each priority rule and each travel demand instances are investigated; and winners and losers of each scenarios were identified. The rules giving priority to the shortest origin-destination distances usually provide with the best results, in terms of total delay generated on the network, independently of the instances.
For partial fulfillment of the requirements of the Civil Engineer at EPFL.

 EPFL, Lausanne, CH optimization modeling (pax assignment) data analysis

gap year

5 Seasons Project (2014-2015)

A year off to travel the world, and to get social and life experiences during 5 seasons. It happened between the Bachelor and the Master's degrees, and was sequenced as follows:

  • Season 1: Transmongolian train, from Moscow to Beijing, via Ulan-Bator. 42 days, 7 time zones, 4 different english accents.
  • Season 2: Back to Europe to work in an Architecture office, Lyon (FR). Architectes Ingénieurs Associés AIA.
  • Season 3: Back to Asia for a back-pack trip (Philipins, Thailand, Malaysia, Myanmar, Nepal)
  • Season 4: Humanitarian project in India. Together with a friend, I joined the NGO Embracing the World that works on the field for a few years to empower women in the villages. This experience wouldn't have been possible without the support of the EPFL association Ingénieurs du Monde.
  • Season 5: Trip in New England (USA) and Eastern Canada, starting from New York City to present the living-lab project we came up with in India.
More info here: 5seasonsproject.com. Travelled with my tripmates and friends Arnaud and Alexandre.
